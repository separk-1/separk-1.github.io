Tackling Data Scarcity in Sensing for Construction Safety: Generation and Adaptation


While building large-scale datasets, I realized that constructing datasets demands a significant amount of time and resources.
Inspired by this, I have conceptualized research in Human Activity Recognition (HAR) based on Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) data.
My idea centers on generating virtual humans using text-based motion generation models like MotionDiffuse, which would involve collecting angular velocity and acceleration data from these virtual entities.
This theoretical approach could significantly reduce the need for manual labeling and enable the generation of a diverse data range, including accident scenarios.

However, as virtual data may not always align with actual field data, I envisage applying my domain adaptation technology to potentially enhance performance in real-world environments.
My ultimate goal with this concept is to address the issue of data scarcity in the construction field and advocate for the future deployment of AI models in real-world construction sites.
Additionally, this idea includes integrating text, image, and IMU data, aiming to pave the way for utilizing a variety of data types in the construction industry.
